[DAILY SHORT — 16_negativity_bias]
Ten people say something nice about you. One person says something rude. Which one are you still thinking about tonight? You will replay that single comment for hours. The ten kind ones? Already gone. It is called negativity bias. Your brain weighs bad more heavily than good, and it is not a flaw. It was built to notice danger before comfort, because missing something bad mattered far more than missing something good. So your mind became a threat detector that never fully clocks out. That sting you can't shake is not a personality flaw. It is the price of a brain built to protect you before it ever learned to make you happy.

That "close door" button in the elevator? It does almost nothing. <break time="0.3s"/> Most were quietly disconnected decades ago, when safety laws forced the doors to wait a fixed time. The button just stayed — as a placebo. <break time="0.3s"/> Same with most crosswalk buttons in big cities. Same with the thermostat in a lot of offices. <break time="0.3s"/> They're left there for one reason: pushing a button tricks your brain into feeling in control. Follow — become a Stan, there's a new one every day.

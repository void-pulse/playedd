# Episode Idea — Could Aliens Actually Reach Us?
Date: 2026-06-09

- **Vein:** Hidden Scale / cosmic (rotates away from ep6/ep7 self + ep8 figure). Pairs with the existential/cosmic bench (simulation, etc.).
- **Working title:** Could Aliens Actually Reach Us? (alts: "Why Aliens Can't Reach Us", "How Far Away Is the Nearest Alien?")
- **Thumbnail (3 distinct, A/B):** (a) tiny Stan on Earth waving at a distant alien on a far planet, huge starry gap between, caps "TOO FAR?"; (b) a rocket pointed at a star with "77,000 YEARS" stamped on it; (c) Stan looking up through a telescope at a faint alien, caps "SO CLOSE?".
- **Hook (cold open):** The odds say aliens probably exist somewhere. So the real question: even if they do, could they ever actually get HERE? Stan looks up expecting yes.
- **The reframe (it's not X, it's Y):** The sky isn't silent because the universe is empty. It's silent because it's so unimaginably vast that even light strains to cross it. Everyone's marooned on their own island. Not alone, just impossibly far apart.
- **EMOTIONAL REGISTER:** neutral + wonder, land on AWE not loneliness/doom. The bigness is astonishing, not sad. (See brand/VOICE.md.)
- **Verified facts (see 01_script.md SOURCES):** Proxima Centauri 4.24 ly; nothing with mass can hit light speed (infinite energy); Voyager would take ~77,000 yrs to reach Proxima (Helios II speed ~18,000 yrs); Voyager after ~48-50 yrs is only ~1 light-day out while the nearest star is ~1,500+ light-days away; Breakthrough Starshot (laser light-sails to ~20% c → ~20 yrs, project stalled); Alcubierre warp drive needs negative-energy/exotic matter we can't make; Fermi paradox (1950, "where is everybody?").
- **MYTH-RISK:** "aliens exist" = state as odds/likely (Drake is wildly uncertain), not fact. Warp drive = theoretical, needs matter that may not exist; don't imply it's coming. Keep "tens of thousands of years" framing accurate.
- **Voice:** Jack (klOYed7ULfdFRLhrPHYE). **Character:** Stan (the stargazer everyman, light touch).
- **Target runtime:** ~8 min (~1,250-1,400 words).

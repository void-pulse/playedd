# Storyboard — Could Aliens Actually Reach Us?

Creative direction for the image track. Worker expands into per-timestamp scenes after audio is locked, following these anchors. Stan (brand/CHARACTER.md) is the stargazer everyman, light touch; the real star of the visuals is the SCALE.

## Style (non-negotiable)
- STYLE_BLOCK from brand/IMAGE_STYLE.md (crude flat colored doodle, thick wobbly lines, no 3D/shading). For space, a flat dark/navy background with simple star dots is allowed (it's the one place a dark bg fits). One bold idea per frame, lots of breathing room. 16:9; ~1 image / 2.3s.

## Beat anchors (key frames)

**[COLD OPEN] Stan wondering**
- Stan standing on a little hill at night, looking up at a sky of simple star dots, a thought bubble with a tiny question-mark alien. Wondering, expectant. (Do NOT reveal the answer.)

**[SETUP] the nearest neighbor**
- A single star labeled "NEAREST STAR" with a tag "4 LIGHT-YEARS." A simple light-beam line traveling from it to a tiny Earth. Caption idea: "the light left 4 years ago."

**[THE TURN] we can't go light speed**
- A doodle rocket slamming into a wall labeled "LIGHT SPEED," a red X + "= INFINITE ENERGY." Cut: the same rocket with a tag "FASTEST PROBE = 77,000 YEARS" pointed at the far star.

**[ESCALATE] the Voyager gut-punch**
- Tiny Voyager probe just outside Earth; a measuring line shows "50 YEARS = 1 LIGHT-DAY," and a vastly longer line off-frame labeled "NEAREST STAR = 1,500+ LIGHT-DAYS." The scale gap is the whole point.

**[REVEAL] aliens would need magic tech**
- A simple alien saucer facing the same huge gap. Two options drawn: "NEAR LIGHT SPEED = impossible energy" and a little warp-bubble ship labeled "WARP DRIVE: needs matter that may not exist." A tiny laser-sail probe tagged "our best idea: 20 yrs... and it stalled."

**[HISTORY] Fermi's question**
- A scientist (doodle) at a lunch table, looking up at an empty star sky, big hand-lettered "WHERE IS EVERYBODY?" + a small "1950" tag.

**[HUMAN LAYER] marooned neighbors**
- Several little planets, each with a tiny waving figure on it, separated by huge dark gulfs, none able to reach the others. Caption: "neighbors who can never visit."

**[PAYOFF] we can't reach them either**
- Stan on Earth with one tiny Voyager dot barely off the planet, the enormous starfield beyond untouched. Caption: "we're not reaching them either."

**[BUTTON] awe, not loneliness**
- Stan looking up, small but calm and awed, under an enormous, beautiful, star-filled sky. Caption: "not alone. just very far apart." (Land on wonder.)
- Then: end card (main video) = silent like/subscribe doodle, LOWER-LEFT only, center + top-right CLEAR (brand/FORMATS.md). No CTA text on the main video.

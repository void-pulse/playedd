# Episode Idea — Why Haven't We Been Back to the Moon?
Date: 2026-06-15

- **Vein:** Hidden Scale / space. Rotates off ep15 (Everyday/Engineered). The "it feels close, the reason we stopped isn't what you think" angle.
- **Working title:** Why Haven't We Been Back to the Moon? (alts: "Why Did We Stop Going to the Moon?", "We Forgot How to Go to the Moon?")
- **Thumbnails (3 distinct, A/B):**
  - (a) Stan looking up at the Moon, a giant footprint on it, big caps "SINCE 1972?" (the half-century gap).
  - (b) A dusty Saturn V rocket in a museum behind a velvet rope, caps "LOST THE TECH?" (the head-fake we break).
  - (c) The Moon with a price tag hanging off it, caps "WHO PAYS?" (the real reframe — money/will, not ability).
- **Hook (cold open):** Twelve people have walked on the Moon. The last one left in 1972. We just flew humans around it again this year, for the first time in fifty years, and STILL nobody has set foot there. **Withhold:** the reason we stopped is not the one everyone repeats.
- **The reframe (it's not X, it's Y):** We didn't lose the ability. We lost the reason. Apollo was never really about the Moon, it was about beating the Soviet Union. The day we won, the multi-billion-dollar reason to keep going evaporated. It's not a technology problem. It's a motivation-and-money problem.
- **Head-fake:** open on the popular answer ("we lost the technology / NASA lost the blueprints"), then break it. The blueprints exist (microfilm at Marshall, the Federal Archives, Rocketdyne's retention program). What's actually gone is the industrial base and the hands-on know-how, and even that is rebuildable. So "we lost the tech" is wrong.
- **EMOTIONAL REGISTER:** neutral + wonder, lands on awe + a little wistful ("the only thing that ever got us there was a reason big enough"). NOT cynical, NOT conspiracy, NOT doom. Concrete (budgets, dates, the Cold War), no Moon-hoax stuff except a one-line, calm dismissal of the radiation myth if it fits.
- **The fun history thread (Logan likes this):** the Cold War drove the whole thing. And the kicker: JFK, the face of "we choose to go to the Moon," privately wasn't that into space. On a 1962 White House tape he tells NASA chief James Webb "I'm not that interested in space." In 1963, at the UN, he openly proposed a JOINT Moon mission WITH the Soviets. The race we remember as destiny was, to the man who launched it, mostly about beating the other guy.
- **Verified science/history (see 01_script.md SOURCES):**
  - 12 humans walked on the Moon, all between 1969 (Apollo 11) and Dec 1972 (Apollo 17, Cernan + Schmitt). Cernan = last man on the Moon.
  - Apollo cost ~$25.8B (1960-73), ~$257-309B in 2025 dollars. At its 1966 peak NASA was ~4.4% of the entire federal budget; today it's ~0.4%.
  - Saturn V blueprints NOT lost — stored on microfilm at Marshall Space Flight Center, ~2,900 cubic feet of Saturn docs at the Federal Archives (East Point, GA), plus Rocketdyne's Knowledge Retention Program on the F-1/J-2 engines. What was lost: the manufacturing base, specialized suppliers, and hands-on institutional expertise (engineers retired).
  - Saturn V production ended; the factories/tooling were dismantled and repurposed (Skylab, Shuttle).
  - Artemis II: crewed lunar flyby, launched Apr 1 2026, returned Apr 10 2026 — first humans around the Moon in 50+ years (no landing). Artemis III re-planned as a lander test; the next crewed LANDING is now Artemis IV, ~2028.
  - Van Allen radiation belts were NOT a barrier (Apollo crews transited quickly); radiation is a real challenge for LONG stays, not the reason we stopped. (Calm one-liner only.)
- **MYTH-RISK:**
  - Do NOT say "NASA lost the blueprints / we forgot how" as fact — that's the head-fake we explicitly debunk.
  - Apollo cost: "more than 250 billion dollars in today's money" (range 257-309B; use conservative).
  - Don't state Artemis IV date as locked ("currently slated for around 2028 / has slipped before").
  - No Moon-hoax engagement beyond a one-line, neutral dismissal of the radiation myth (optional). Keep it wonder, not debunk-war.
  - JFK tape + UN joint-mission proposal are real/declassified; present as documented.

# Storyboard — Why Do You Get Déjà Vu?

Creative direction for the image track. Worker expands into per-timestamp scenes after audio is locked. Colored flat-scene style (brand/IMAGE_STYLE.md). Follow the ANTI-PATTERNS: one bold idea per frame, NO cross-out-AND-restate (show the idea, don't label a negation of a label), use Stan sparingly (not every frame), plenty of pure-concept frames.

## Beat anchors (key frames)

**[COLD OPEN] the wave hits**
- Stan at a party in a stranger's colored living room, a shiver running down his neck, a faint translucent "echo" of the same scene layered just behind him. One idea: the uncanny "I've been here" jolt.

**[SETUP] the double feeling**
- A single moment split down the middle: one half tagged "NEW," the other half tagged "SEEN THIS BEFORE," the same scene in both. The paradox in one image.

**[HISTORY / HEAD-FAKE] the old explanations**
- A ghostly past-era version of a person standing behind them ("a past life?").
- A crystal ball / dream showing the same scene ("a prophecy?"). Keep each to one idea; these are the explanations we'll move past.

**[THE TURN] two separate systems**
- A brain with two clearly separate departments: a "MEMORY" filing cabinet on one side, and a standalone "FAMILIAR" stamp/alarm on the other. They're different machines.

**[REVEAL 1] the misfire**
- The "FAMILIAR" stamp slamming down hard onto a totally blank, empty file folder. The feeling with nothing behind it.

**[REVEAL 2] jamais vu**
- The word "DOOR" written over and over down the frame, the last one dissolving into alien squiggles. The dial swinging the other way (fun, one idea).

**[REVEAL 3] same floor plan**
- Two simple rooms side by side with the exact same layout outline overlaid in red. "Same shape, can't place it."

**[REVEAL 4] the brain catching itself**
- A little inspector/fact-checker inside the brain holding up a magnifying glass to a false "FAMILIAR" alarm, busting it. One idea: the brain catching its own error (this is the payoff visual).

**[HUMAN LAYER] more when young**
- A simple down-slope line from a young figure to an older figure, déjà vu sparks thinning out along the way. A sign of a sharp, active young memory system.

**[PAYOFF] keeping itself honest**
- Show the idea, not a negation: a calm brain giving itself a small green check after the false alarm. (Do NOT draw "past life" crossed out plus "not a past life" written, just show the brain resolving it cleanly.)

**[BUTTON] the shiver passes**
- Stan back at the party, the echo behind him fading, a small knowing smile. Calm, a little wonder.
- Then: end card (main video) = silent like/subscribe doodle, LOWER-LEFT only, center + top-right CLEAR. No CTA text on the main video.

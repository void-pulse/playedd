# Episode Idea — Why Do You Get Déjà Vu?
Date: 2026-06-10

- **Vein:** Your Own Mind (the mind cluster). Spaced from ep10 Thoughts by ep11 Tired (rotation OK).
- **Working title:** Why Do You Get Déjà Vu? (alts: "What Is Déjà Vu, Really?")
- **Hook (cold open):** Stan walks into a stranger's house he's never been in and is dead certain he's lived this exact moment before. He hasn't. Why?
- **The reframe (it's not X, it's Y):** It's not a past life or a glimpse of the future. It's a familiarity signal misfiring with no memory behind it, and quite possibly the feeling of your brain catching its own error in real time.
- **EMOTIONAL REGISTER:** neutral + wonder, reassuring ("your brain keeping itself honest"). NOT spooky/woo (don't endorse past-life/precognition), NOT alarming.
- **The fun history thread:** for centuries déjà vu was taken as proof of reincarnation or prophecy ("a glitch in the matrix" is the modern version). The term was only coined ~1900 by Émile Boirac; the science is recent.
- **Verified facts (see 01_script.md SOURCES):** familiarity vs recollection are separate systems (rhinal/medial temporal lobe can misfire a "familiar" flag with no memory); gestalt/spatial-resemblance can trigger it (Cleary); O'Connor fMRI = frontal conflict-detection regions light up, not memory regions → déjà vu may be the brain DETECTING a false familiarity (leading theory, not settled); peaks in young adults and declines with age (sign of a healthy, self-auditing system); jamais vu / semantic satiation = the inverse glitch; temporal-lobe epilepsy aura → responsible medical caveat.
- **MYTH-RISK:** fact-check theory = leading, not proven (déjà vu is hard to study). Don't endorse the supernatural framings. Medical caveat responsible (ordinary déjà vu is healthy; only frequent/prolonged + other symptoms → doctor). Don't alarm.

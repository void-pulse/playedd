# Episode Idea — Why Does Time Speed Up as You Get Older?
Date: 2026-06-08

- **Vein:** Your Own Mind/Reality (YOM). The human/perception lane (channel spine). See memory: human-subject lane.
- **Working title:** Why Does Time Speed Up as You Get Older? (alts: "Why Does Time Go Faster Every Year?", "Where Did the Year Go?")
- **Thumbnail (one bold idea):** a calendar with pages blowing away, or a clock with kid on one side / adult on other. Big caps: "WHERE'D IT GO?" High contrast, one idea.
- **Hook (cold open):** A single summer as a kid felt endless. Now a whole year vanishes in what feels like weeks. Same 365 days. Why does it keep speeding up?
- **The reframe (it's not X, it's Y):** Time isn't accelerating. Your brain just records less of it when life turns to routine, so it shrinks in hindsight. And you can slow it back down with novelty.
- **Head-fake (one per episode):** open on the "obvious" proportional-theory answer (a year is a smaller fraction of your life), then break it: it doesn't fit how time actually feels. The real reason is memory.
- **EMOTIONAL REGISTER:** neutral + wonder, with a gently empowering payoff. CRITICAL: avoid the mortality/"life is slipping away" downer. Keep it about perception + memory + what you can do. (See brand/VOICE.md.)
- **Verified science (see 01_script.md SOURCES):** proportional theory (Janet 1877, weak alone); memory/novelty-encoding theory (the core: brain gauges past duration by density of distinct memories; childhood = firsts = dense; adult routine = automaticity = compressed); Bejan 2019 (processing slows with age) present as one debated idea; consensus = multifactor; novelty demonstrably stretches perceived time.
- **MYTH-RISK:** don't claim a single cause; proportional theory is the "obvious but weak" one (use as head-fake). Label Bejan's processing theory as debated. The novelty-slows-time payoff is well supported.

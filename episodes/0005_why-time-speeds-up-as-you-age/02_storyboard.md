# Storyboard — Why Does Time Speed Up as You Get Older?

Creative direction for the image track. The worker expands these into per-timestamp scenes after the audio is locked and timestamped, but **every frame must follow the anchors below**.

## Style (non-negotiable)
- Pull STYLE_BLOCK from brand/IMAGE_STYLE.md (crude MS-Paint doodle, thick wobbly outlines, stick figures, flat colors, mostly empty).
- **ONE bold visual metaphor per frame.** Iconic, high-contrast, meaningful. NOT literal scene-clutter. If you can't name the single image in four words, it's too busy.
- Subtle color, evolution-up (per IMAGE_STYLE color direction). Clean, big, readable on a phone.
- 16:9 landscape for the main video. Short = native 9:16 (`--portrait`).
- Cadence ~one image every ~2.3s; the assembler applies SYNC_OFFSET_SEC 0.25. Hold longer on slow lines, quick cuts on fast ones (see FORMATS cut rhythm). Use the anchors below as the key frames; fill coverage between them with simple variations on the same metaphor, never new clutter.

## Beat anchors (the key frames)

**[COLD OPEN] kid summer endless → year gone**
- A small stick kid lying back in tall grass under a huge slow sun. Big hand-lettered "SUMMER" stretched very wide. Lazy, endless.
- Cut: an adult stick figure as calendar pages blast off the wall and fly away. "DECEMBER?!" Fast, gone.

**[SETUP] everyone feels it**
- Split frame: little kid pointing down a very long road toward a tiny far-off sign "SUMMER". | Adult shrugging at a blank wall calendar, question mark over head.

**[THE TURN] the obvious answer, then wrong**
- A simple life-bar / pie: age 5 = one BIG shaded slice. Age 50 = a thin sliver. Tidy.
- Cut: a big red X stamped over the pie. "MOSTLY WRONG."

**[REVEAL 1] brain counts memories, not clocks**
- A doodle brain. A clock crossed out beside it. A stack of little polaroid photos glowing inside/under the brain. Label: "MEMORIES = TIME."

**[REVEAL 2] childhood = firsts, dense**
- A kid stick figure surrounded by bright glowing polaroids, each labeled tiny: "1st bike", "ocean", "school". Packed, colorful, full.

**[REVEAL 3] adult routine = compression**
- A tall stack of identical gray boxes all labeled "TUESDAY", collapsing/squishing down into one thin flat line. "SAME. SAME. SAME."

**[REVEAL 4] the debated second idea (Bejan)**
- A kid's eye firing many little snapshots per second vs an adult eye firing few. A small "?" in the corner to flag it's debated. Keep it light, one frame or two.

**[HUMAN LAYER] not slipping away, just unwritten**
- A diary/journal open: a few pages dense with doodles, most pages blank. Hand resting. "NOT GONE. JUST BLANK." Calm, not sad.

**[PAYOFF] novelty slows it back down**
- A stick figure stepping OFF a gray treadmill toward a bright glowing doorway marked "NEW".
- Cut: one big glowing polaroid "1 WEEK SOMEWHERE NEW" next to three thin gray bars "3 MONTHS SAME". The big one dwarfs them.

**[BUTTON] you have a say**
- Two paths from one stick figure: a plain gray straight path, and a "long way" path lit with little glowing firsts. The figure steps toward the lit one.
- Final frame: the silent **Playedd logo** (end stamp). No CTA text.

## Thumbnail (one bold idea)
- A wall calendar with its pages blowing off into the air, or a clock with pages flying. Big bold caps: **"WHERE'D IT GO?"**. High contrast, solid bold background + selective pop color (ep3-v3 level). One idea only.
- Alt to A/B: kid-side vs adult-side split, big "FASTER?" caps.

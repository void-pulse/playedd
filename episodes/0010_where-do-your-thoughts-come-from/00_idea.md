# Episode Idea — Where Do Your Thoughts Come From?
Date: 2026-06-09

- **Vein:** Your Own Mind / big human question (the existential-mind cluster). First of the mind run; space the others (déjà vu, why-are-you-you) across later slots.
- **Working title:** Where Do Your Thoughts Come From? (alts: "Who's Thinking Your Thoughts?", "You Don't Choose Your Next Thought")
- **Thumbnails (3 distinct, A/B):** (a) Stan with a thought bubble appearing out of a "?" cloud, caps "WHO SENT THAT?"; (b) Stan's head with a little mail-slot, a thought-letter sliding out, caps "YOU DIDN'T WRITE IT?"; (c) the history beat: a brain in a trash can next to a heart on a pedestal, caps "WRONG ORGAN?".
- **Hook (cold open):** Try to guess your NEXT thought before you think it. You can't. It just shows up. So who sent it?
- **The reframe (it's not X, it's Y):** You feel like the author of your thoughts. You're actually closer to the audience, they arise from a hidden process and you're the one who notices.
- **EMOTIONAL REGISTER:** neutral + wonder, lands gently LIBERATING (you're not your thoughts; you choose which to act on). NOT free-will-doom, NOT nihilistic, NOT woo. Keep it concrete (DMN, the studies, the history), not mystical.
- **The fun history thread (Logan likes this):** for almost all of history we didn't even know thoughts came from the brain. Egyptians scooped the brain out through the nose and threw it away during mummification (heart = seat of thought). Aristotle thought the brain was a radiator that cooled the blood and you thought with your heart.
- **Verified science/history (see 01_script.md SOURCES):** thoughts arise unbidden (you can't pre-think a thought); the Default Mode Network generates spontaneous thought/mind-wandering at rest; mind-wandering ~47% of waking life (Killingsworth & Gilbert 2010, Harvard, ~2,250 people); Libet readiness potential (~300ms brain activity before conscious decision) — HOTLY DEBATED, only simple motor acts, Libet himself rejected the "predetermined" reading (the "veto"); Egyptians discarded the brain / heart = intelligence; Aristotle brain-as-cooling-radiator (cardiocentric).
- **MYTH-RISK:** Do NOT conclude "you have no free will." Libet is debated, applies to simple twitches, and even Libet disagreed with the hard reading. Frame as "the brain often acts first," then reaffirm agency over which thoughts to act on. Keep "you don't author your thoughts" (well-supported) distinct from "you have no control" (overreach).

- **Target runtime:** ~8 min (~1,300-1,400 words).

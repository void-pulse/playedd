# ep11 Storyboard — Why Are You Tired All the Time?

Visual anchors, one bold idea per frame, Stan featured throughout. Colored/simple-scene STYLE_BLOCK (flat crude doodle, NO 3D). Stan on-model (round white head, two dot eyes, simple line mouth, thin line body, no hair). 16:9. Build `04_scenes.json` per-timestamp from these anchors at build time.

## Mood arc (for the ducked score)
curious/relatable (cold open) → "huh, two clocks" intrigue (reveals 1–2) → bright understanding (light) → the click of the reframe (social jetlag) → warm/cozy (history) → calm, reassuring lift (button).

## Beat anchors (one bold idea per frame)

**COLD OPEN**
- Stan face-down asleep on an office desk, big wall clock reading 3:00, three little "z"s. He's slightly grey/green = wiped.
- Cut: Stan flat on his back in bed at night, eyes WIDE open, phone glowing on his face, wall clock at 12:00.
- A sleep-tracker watch on Stan's wrist proudly showing "8 HRS" — and Stan still looks dead.

**HEAD-FAKE**
- A thought bubble over Stan: "just sleep more" — with a big red X over it.
- Two other little Stans snoozing 9 and 10 hours, still dragging — quantity isn't it.
- A single bold "2 SYSTEMS?" with two question-mark clocks facing different ways.

**REVEAL 1 — sleep pressure (the hourglass)**
- Stan shaped like an hourglass, sand trickling into the bottom as a sun arcs across the sky behind him (building all day).
- Night: the hourglass full to the top, Stan's eyelids heavy.
- Coffee cup slapping a strip of tape over a little eyeball/gauge labeled with a "z" — caffeine taping over the sensor.

**REVEAL 2 — the body clock + timetable**
- A tiny clock the size of a grain of rice glowing behind Stan's eyes inside a simple head cross-section.
- A clock face with a drawn alertness line over the day: a morning peak, a deep dip at 2–4, a little bump at night. Stan riding the line like a roller coaster.
- Afternoon: Stan melting into "soup" at his desk at 3:00, a sandwich beside him shrugging with a speech bubble "not me."
- Night: Stan in bed at 11:00 wide awake, a lightbulb of "ideas" over his head, buzzing.

**REVEAL 3 — light sets the clock**
- A bright morning sun beaming a ray straight into Stan's eye, the little rice-clock locking into place ("CLICK").
- A close-up eye with a tiny antenna/sensor (not for seeing) pointed at the light.
- Night: Stan in bed holding his glowing phone like a tiny sun inches from his face; behind him the clock's hands being shoved backward.

**REVEAL 4 — social jetlag (the reframe)**
- Stan slumped in an airplane seat, seatbelt on, big caps "SOCIAL JETLAG" — but the window shows his own bedroom, not the sky.
- A week strip: Mon–Fri an alarm clock yanking Stan up early; Sat–Sun Stan sleeping in late and happy.
- A map with a little plane flying WEST on Saturday and EAST on Monday — over Stan's house, never leaving.
- Three Stans standing in a row, two of them yawning — "MORE THAN 2 IN 3."
- Stan drawn as a perfectly good wall clock, next to a crumpled "9-to-5" schedule that doesn't match him: "OUT OF SYNC, NOT BROKEN."

**HISTORY THREAD — segmented sleep**
- A cozy candlelit old bedroom: Stan sitting up awake at midnight between two beds labeled "1st SLEEP" and "2nd SLEEP," calm, tending a candle.
- Tiny vignette: old-timey figures praying / poking a fire / chatting in the dark midnight gap.
- A glowing Edison lightbulb erasing the "midnight gap" off a sleep timeline (one block left).
- Stan at 3am in modern bed, calm, a little ghost of his great-great-grandfather giving a thumbs up.

**BUTTON**
- Stan as a friendly wind-up clock being set to the "wrong time" by a giant calendar/office hand.
- Final: Stan wide awake at midnight, energy sparkles around him, but smiling now (he gets it).
- Tiny responsible aside: a small doctor's clipboard with a heart, light touch, "if it never lifts → doctor."
- CUT TO SILENT PLAYEDD LOGO (no slogan).

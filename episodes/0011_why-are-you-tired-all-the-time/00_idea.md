# Episode Idea — Why Are You Tired All the Time?
Date: 2026-06-10

- **Vein:** Your Own Body / energy — the **flagship "Why Are You…?" series** (body/energy branch, distinct register from the mind episodes). Safe high-search layup. Follows ep10 (Mind/Thoughts); rotates off the mind sub-vein onto body/energy.
- **Working title:** Why Are You Tired All the Time? (alts: "Why Are You Always Tired?", "Tired Even After 8 Hours?")
- **Thumbnails (3 distinct, A/B):**
  - (a) Stan face-down asleep on a desk under a wall clock reading 3:00, big caps "8 HOURS?!" (the paradox: slept a full night, still dead by afternoon).
  - (b) Stan in bed at night, eyes wide open, phone glowing on his face, clock on the wall at 12:00, caps "WIDE AWAKE?" (the night second-wind).
  - (c) Stan drawn as a wind-up clock, an alarm clock yanking him by a string, caps "WRONG TIME ZONE?" (the social-jetlag reframe).
- **Hook (cold open):** Stan got a full eight hours, did everything right, and he's still a zombie by 3pm and wide awake at midnight. Why? **Withhold:** the reason usually isn't sleep at all.
- **The reframe (it's not X, it's Y):** You're not short on sleep. You're **out of sync**. Tiredness isn't (mostly) about how MUCH you sleep, it's about WHEN — your internal clock and your schedule are set to different time zones. You're jet-lagged without leaving the house ("social jetlag").
- **Head-fake:** open on the obvious fix ("just sleep more"), then break it — plenty of people get their 8–10 hours and still drag. So it's not the amount.
- **EMOTIONAL REGISTER:** neutral + wonder, lands **reassuring / "it's not your fault, you're not broken, you're out of sync."** NOT a health-advice video, NOT shaming, NOT doom. Reveal the MECHANISM, not a regimen. One light, responsible medical caveat (persistent weeks-long fatigue can be thyroid/iron/sleep apnea — see a doctor), per the "Why Are You…?" treatment rule.
- **The fun history thread (Logan likes this):** for most of human history people slept in TWO shifts — a "first sleep" and a "second sleep" — waking on purpose around midnight for an hour or two (chores, prayer, talk, sex, visiting). Electric light squeezed sleep into one block and we forgot the gap existed. Reframes the "I woke at 3am, something's wrong with me" panic. (Ekirch — note it's debated, don't overstate.)
- **Verified science/history (see 01_script.md SOURCES):**
  - **Two-process model** (Borbély): Process S = sleep pressure from **adenosine** building while awake, cleared by sleep; caffeine **blocks the adenosine sensor**, doesn't remove the pressure. Process C = the circadian clock, independent of how long you've been awake.
  - **Circadian schedule** (Process C): mid-morning alertness peak, **afternoon dip ~2–4pm** (the clock, not lunch), and an evening **"wake-maintenance zone" / second wind** that fights sleep before bed.
  - **Master clock = suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN)**, ~20,000 neurons in the hypothalamus, smaller than a grain of rice. **Light is its master signal** via melanopsin retinal cells; morning light anchors/advances the clock, evening/blue light **suppresses melatonin and delays** it.
  - **Social jetlag** (Roenneberg, coined 2006): the gap between biological clock (chronotype) and social schedule; **>2 in 3 people** carry ≥1 hour of it per week; evening types worst. Book: "Internal Time: Chronotypes, Social Jet Lag, and Why You're So Tired."
  - **Segmented sleep** (Ekirch): "first sleep"/"second sleep" with a midnight waking interval, pre-industrial; references back to Homer/Virgil; consolidated sleep is recent. DEBATED — flag it.
- **MYTH-RISK:**
  - Do NOT give prescriptive health advice or claim the science "cures" tiredness. Reveal mechanism only.
  - Social-jetlag stat: say "more than two out of three people" carry ≥1h/week (Roenneberg-lab data), not "everyone."
  - Segmented sleep is DEBATED — "historians think / the record suggests," not settled fact.
  - Afternoon dip: "mostly your clock, not the sandwich" — don't claim lunch has zero effect.
  - Caffeine: "blocks the signal," does not erase the tiredness — accurate.
  - Light/melatonin: "suppresses melatonin and delays your clock" — accurate; don't say it "blinds" the clock.
  - Keep the medical caveat light and responsible; don't pathologize ordinary tiredness, but do say weeks-long fatigue warrants a doctor.

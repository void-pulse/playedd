# Storyboard — Why Do You Procrastinate?

Creative direction for the image track. Worker expands into per-timestamp scenes after audio is locked, following these anchors. Stan (brand/CHARACTER.md) is the everyman throughout, keep him consistent and on-model.

## Style (non-negotiable)
- STYLE_BLOCK from brand/IMAGE_STYLE.md (crude flat colored doodle, thick wobbly lines, no 3D/shading). Colored simple scenes (locked look), ONE bold idea per frame, lots of breathing room.
- Stan = same simple stick figure every frame, expressive with two lines.
- 16:9 main; ~1 image / 2.3s, holds on slow lines, quick cuts on fast ones.

## Beat anchors (key frames, Stan featured)

**[COLD OPEN] Stan busy-avoiding**
- Stan frantically scrubbing a dish, sweating, while behind him a glowing task labeled "DUE TOMORROW" sits ignored. Busy, but tormented.
- Cut: split frame: a relaxed figure flopped on a couch labeled "LAZY" (calm) vs tense Stan surrounded by chores labeled "PROCRASTINATING" (suffering). One idea: "NOT THE SAME."

**[SETUP] the usual advice fails**
- A planner, a clock, a "TRY HARDER" poster, all stamped with a red X. The standard fix that never works.

**[THE TURN] it's a feeling, not the task**
- The task drawn as a glowing blob radiating a little storm cloud of bad feelings: "DREAD," "BORED," "WHAT IF IT'S BAD." Stan leaning away from the cloud, not the task.
- Cut: Stan turning away from the task and getting an instant relief bubble ("ahhh") NOW, while a small IOU/credit card slip drops into a mailbox labeled "FUTURE STAN."

**[ESCALATE] opposite of lazy + future self**
- Present-Stan calmly handing a tiny ticking bomb to a slightly-faded "FUTURE STAN" who looks alarmed. Relief now, panic later.
- Cut: a clock at 2:00 AM, future-Stan wide awake and panicking at the desk.

**[HISTORY TURN] akrasia (the Greeks)**
- An ancient Greek scene: a toga figure (Stan in a toga) holding a scroll labeled "AKRASIA," two philosophers arguing beside him. Caption idea: "knowing the smart thing. not doing it." Ancient puzzle.

**[HISTORY — the word]**
- Big bold hand-lettered "PRO" + "CRAS" with a little = and "TO TOMORROW." A calendar page flipping endlessly to a day stamped "TOMORROW." The word is a confession.

**[HUMAN LAYER] guilt is the fuel**
- Stan beating himself up (little storm over his head, "LAZY? USELESS?"), and that guilt visibly piling MORE weight onto the task, which grows bigger and scarier. One idea: guilt = gasoline (a small flame fed by a guilt can).

**[PAYOFF] forgive, then shrink**
- Stan setting down a heavy "GUILT" weight with a small "it's okay"; the scary giant task immediately shrinks back to a normal, approachable size.
- Cut: a giant first stair labeled "START" breaking into one tiny step labeled "ONE BAD SENTENCE." Make the first step too small to be scary.

**[BUTTON] Stan catches himself**
- Stan back at the spotless kitchen sink, about to scrub again, but pausing, setting the sponge down, calm and self-aware this time (no guilt cloud). At peace.
- Then: end card (main video) = silent like/subscribe doodle, LOWER-LEFT only, center + top-right CLEAR (brand/FORMATS.md). No CTA text on the main video.

## Thumbnail (one bold idea)
- Stan frantically scrubbing/cleaning while a glowing "DUE TOMORROW" task is ignored behind him. Big bold caps "NOT LAZY?" + small red "?". High-contrast solid background. One idea. Stan as the face of the series.

# Episode Idea — Why Do You Procrastinate?
Date: 2026-06-09

- **Vein:** "Why Do You [trait]?" flagship self-question. Pairs with ep6 (Lazy) as a binge combo. See brand/STRATEGY.md.
- **Working title:** Why Do You Procrastinate? (alts: "Why Can't You Just Start?", "Procrastination Isn't Laziness")
- **Thumbnail (one bold idea):** Stan frantically scrubbing dishes / reorganizing while a glowing "DUE TOMORROW" task sits ignored behind him. Big caps "NOT LAZY?" High contrast.
- **Hook (cold open):** Stan has a deadline tomorrow. He sits down to start, then suddenly cleans the whole kitchen instead. He WANTS to do it. He keeps not doing it. And he feels awful the whole time.
- **The reframe (it's not X, it's Y):** Procrastination is NOT laziness and NOT bad time management. It's emotional avoidance, you're dodging the bad FEELING the task gives you (dread, self-doubt, boredom), not the task. It's basically the opposite of lazy.
- **Differentiator from ep6 (critical, so it's not a repeat):** Lazy = brain conserving energy, content doing nothing. Procrastinate = you want to do it, you're often busy doing other things, and you feel terrible. Different mechanism, different history, different fix.
- **The fun history thread:** the ancient Greeks obsessed over this exact puzzle, "akrasia," acting against your own better judgment (Socrates & Aristotle argued how it's even possible). And the word itself is a confession: Latin pro ("forward") + cras ("tomorrow") = "put it to tomorrow."
- **EMOTIONAL REGISTER:** neutral + wonder, reassuring via mechanism. NEVER shame. Key beat: guilt MAKES IT WORSE (more bad feeling on the task = more avoidance). Payoff is self-compassion, not discipline.
- **Verified science/history (see 01_script.md SOURCES):** procrastination = short-term mood repair / emotion regulation, not time management (Sirois & Pychyl 2013); present self vs future self; akrasia (ancient Greek "weakness of will"); etymology pro+cras; self-forgiveness reduces future procrastination (Wohl, Pychyl & Bennett 2010, mediated by reduced negative affect).
- **MYTH-RISK:** say procrastinators are "often" perfectionists, not always. Don't oversell self-forgiveness as a magic cure (it's a real effect, frame as "the closest thing to a cure"). Keep "ancient Greeks" general (Socrates/Aristotle on akrasia is accurate).

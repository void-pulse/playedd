# Episode Idea — Why Are You Lazy?
Date: 2026-06-08

- **Vein:** "Why Are You [trait]?" flagship self-question (the richest relatable sub-vein). See brand/STRATEGY.md.
- **Working title:** Why Are You Lazy? (alts: "Why Are You So Lazy?", "Why Can't You Just Do the Thing?")
- **Thumbnail (one bold idea):** a stick figure sunk deep into a couch, big caps "LAZY?" with a small red question mark. High contrast. (Curious, not shaming.)
- **Hook (cold open):** You had all day. You didn't do it. Then came the guilt. The twist: the guilt is the lie, not the laziness.
- **The reframe (it's not X, it's Y):** You're not broken or weak. Your brain is a million-year-old energy-saving machine doing its job, and the idea that resting is a moral failure is a recent invention.
- **The fun history thread (Logan wants this):** "Laziness as a sin" was literally invented. 4th-c. desert monks named the afternoon slump acedia, "the noonday demon" (Evagrius). It became the deadly sin of sloth. Then the Protestant work ethic + the factory era rebranded resting from normal to moral failure. The guilt in your chest is a few-hundred-year-old sales pitch.
- **EMOTIONAL REGISTER:** neutral + wonder, reassuring via mechanism. NEVER shaming. Reframe the "flaw" as a feature + a manufactured guilt. Small useful lever at the end (make the first step tiny), grounded in the science, not a pep talk. (See brand/VOICE.md.)
- **Verified science/history (see 01_script.md SOURCES):** brain/body energy conservation, "law of least effort" (metabolic accountant; we unconsciously minimize movement energy); evolutionary calorie-scarcity logic; acedia → Evagrius's eight temptations → Gregory the Great's seven deadly sins → "sloth"; Aquinas "sorrow about spiritual good"; Protestant work ethic (Calvin/Weber) moralizing idleness; industrial-era clocks + "living to work."
- **MYTH-RISK:** "law of least effort" is well-supported but say the brain "tends to" minimize effort, not absolute. Protestant-work-ethic = Weber's widely-cited thesis; frame as "a new idea took hold," don't over-claim single causation. AVOID the contested Sahlins "hunter-gatherers barely worked" stat.

- **Target runtime:** ~8 min (~1,200-1,350 words).

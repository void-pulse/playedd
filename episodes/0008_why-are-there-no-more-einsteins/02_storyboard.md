# Storyboard — Why Are There No More Einsteins?

Creative direction for the image track. Worker expands into per-timestamp scenes after audio is locked, following these anchors.

## Characters
- **Stan** (brand/CHARACTER.md): the modern everyman / "us," asking where the geniuses went. Consistent every frame.
- **Doodle-Einstein**: a one-off historical figure for this episode. Simple recognizable doodle: wild hair, bushy mustache, stick body. Keep him recognizable across his frames. (He is NOT Stan.)

## Style (non-negotiable)
- STYLE_BLOCK from brand/IMAGE_STYLE.md (crude flat colored doodle, thick wobbly lines, no 3D/shading). Colored simple scenes, ONE bold idea per frame, breathing room. 16:9 main; ~1 image / 2.3s.

## Beat anchors (key frames)

**[COLD OPEN] the patent clerk who rewrote the universe**
- Doodle-Einstein at a patent-office desk, a stack of boring paperwork on one side, casually scribbling a glowing "E=mc²" on a scrap. Caption: "1905."
- Cut: modern Stan shrugging, scratching his head at a blank thought bubble labeled "living genius?" Can't fill it.

**[SETUP] what he pulled off in one year**
- Three quick bold icons, doodle-Einstein gesturing to them: tiny atoms with a check ("ATOMS ARE REAL"), a beam of light breaking into little particles + a Nobel medal, and a clock + ruler bending ("SPACE + TIME"). Big "E=mc²" stamp. Caption: "ONE YEAR."

**[ESCALATE AWE] it runs your life (GPS)**
- A phone with a blue GPS "you are here" dot, a dotted line linking it back to doodle-Einstein. Red caption: "WITHOUT HIM: OFF BY 2 KM/DAY."

**[THE TURN] the lone-genius myth**
- The classic "lone genius" hero on a poster/pedestal, spotlight on him. Then pull back: he's standing on a big stack of other people's shoulders/books. Caption: "NEVER REALLY ALONE."

**[REVEAL 1] burden of knowledge**
- A mountain. 1905 side: the frontier flag sits low, doodle-Einstein sprints up to it easily. Today side: "base camp" is way up high, a tiny modern climber far below, a huge gap to even start. Caption: "THE CLIMB GOT LONGER."

**[REVEAL 2] teams replaced the hero**
- Split: an old paper with ONE big name ("EINSTEIN") vs a modern paper with a hundred tiny crammed names. Caption: "WHERE'S THE FACE?"

**[DEEPER] born + made + timing + luck**
- Doodle-Einstein in the middle with floating factors: a brain ("BORN"), a clock/calendar ("RIGHT MOMENT"), a sleepy patent-office desk ("TIME TO THINK"), a pair of dice ("LUCK"). Caption: "NOT JUST A BRAIN."

**[HUMAN LAYER] they're still here, just hidden**
- A crowd of modern stick people; a few faintly glowing, standing together in a team far out on a distant frontier line. Stan in front squinting, unable to pick them out. Caption: "THEY'RE STILL HERE."

**[PAYOFF] it takes all of us now**
- Split: old = one lone statue on a pedestal. New = a thousand tiny figures plus a computer, together lifting one giant glowing idea, no statue. Caption: "NOW IT TAKES ALL OF US."

**[BUTTON] more human than ever**
- Stan looking up, calm and a little awed, at the huge crowd-built idea / starry sky. Peaceful, hopeful. Caption: "MORE HUMAN THAN EVER."
- Then: end card (main video) = silent like/subscribe doodle, LOWER-LEFT only, center + top-right CLEAR (brand/FORMATS.md). No CTA text on the main video.

## Thumbnail (one bold idea)
- Stan small, shrugging, looking up at a giant doodle-Einstein silhouette with a big red "?". Bold caps "WHERE'D THEY GO?" High-contrast solid background. One idea.

# Episode Idea — Why Are There No More Einsteins?
Date: 2026-06-09

- **Vein:** "Famous figure as anchor, but really about us" (history + psychology). NOT a biography. See brand/STRATEGY.md + wonder_slate v6.
- **Working title:** Why Are There No More Einsteins? (alts: "Where Did All the Geniuses Go?", "Why Can't You Name a Living Genius?")
- **Thumbnail (one bold idea):** Stan shrugging, looking up at a giant Einstein silhouette with a red "?". Big caps "WHERE'D THEY GO?" High contrast.
- **Hook (cold open):** Quick, name a living genius. A real Einstein of today. ...You can't. Stan can't either. It feels like we used to mint world-changers and then just stopped.
- **The reframe (it's not X, it's Y):** We didn't stop making geniuses. The SHAPE of genius changed, from a lone hero to a hidden crowd, because the frontier got so far away it takes a whole team to reach. The Einsteins are still here; you'll just never know their names.
- **EMOTIONAL REGISTER:** neutral + wonder, ends uplifting. Explicitly reject "humanity got dumber." Not a self-esteem topic, but keep the awe + a hopeful, slightly profound landing.
- **The awe setup (history, the fun part):** 1905, Einstein is 26, a clerk at the Bern patent office, academic career stalled (thesis rejected), doing physics in spare time. In ONE year he proves atoms are real (Brownian motion), explains light as particles (photoelectric effect, his Nobel), and rewrites space + time (special relativity) plus E=mc². And it's not trivia: your GPS would drift ~2 km/day without his relativity.
- **The real reasons (the meat):** (1) the lone-genius narrative was always partly a myth, even Einstein built on others; (2) the "burden of knowledge" (Bloom et al. 2020), the frontier is now so far out it takes decades just to reach it; (3) science became giant teams, breakthroughs have 100 authors, no single face; (4) genius = born AND made AND timing AND luck (partly genetic, but the right field/moment/freedom matters more); Einstein had idle hours at a patent office.
- **Payoff:** the geniuses aren't gone, they're spread out and nameless. The next leap comes from a thousand minds + a machine, no statue. Not decline, evolution. "One person used to be enough to move the world. Now it takes all of us."
- **Verified (see 01_script.md SOURCES):** all above.
- **MYTH-RISK:** don't say Einstein "wasn't a genius" (he was), just not literally alone. Don't pin an exact heritability % (sources vary ~50-75%); say "partly genetic." Burden of knowledge / ideas-harder-to-find = Bloom 2020 thesis, frame as research. Never imply people got dumber.
- **Voice:** Jack (klOYed7ULfdFRLhrPHYE). **Character:** Stan (everyman / audience surrogate asking "where are they?").
- **Target runtime:** ~8 min (~1,250-1,400 words).

# Playedd Story System

Every episode is a mystery, not a lecture. The thesis is always "you're being played, here's how." Retention is the whole game: viewers stay only while a question is open. Engineer the story in the SCRIPT first, before narration or storyboard.

## The Spine (ABT mystery shape)
1. HOOK (0-15s): open inside the mystery the title/thumbnail promised. No intro or throat-clearing. Plant the one big question the episode answers.
2. STAKES ("And"): make it personal and familiar. Put the viewer in the chair as protagonist. "You already know this. You do it anyway."
3. THE TURN ("But"): flip the obvious into a real question. Open the deep loop.
4. ESCALATING REVEALS: layer mechanisms mild to disturbing. Never front-load the best one. Each section closes one loop and opens a bigger one (a rehook). Connective tissue: "but that's not even the clever part," "here's where it gets strange."
5. EMOTIONAL PIVOT: where the fun facts turn human and dark. The retention anchor near the end.
6. PAYOFF ("Therefore"): close the exact loop opened in the hook. Land the thesis.
7. BUTTON: twist it onto the viewer, make it bigger than the topic, end on the brand line "you're being played."

## The Tricks
- Open loops + rehooks: never let the story feel finished; every transition closes one loop and opens a bigger one.
- Make the viewer predict: tease what's coming ("in a second, the trick you've personally fallen for").
- The reframe: the signature "it's not X, it's Y" flip. The shareable intellectual payoff.
- The head-fake: set an expectation, then break it (one per episode).
- Escalation: order reveals mild to disturbing; save the best for late.
- Tension and release: raise a problem, build pressure, resolve, repeat.
- Specificity buys belief: real names, studies, numbers. Makes it feel uncovered, not invented.
- Make it personal: "you/your," viewer is the protagonist, emotional stakes.

## Retention Diagnostics (read the curve after each upload)
- Early drop (first 2 min): tighten the open; the value/mystery must be unmistakable in 30s.
- Mid-video dip: add a twist or rehook there.
- End decline: strengthen the payoff and button.
- Target: hold the curve high. ~70% gets promoted, ~30% gets buried.

## Process
1. Script as a retention machine: lock the spine, loops, escalation order, reframe, and button BEFORE narration.
2. Narration carries the story; the storyboard maps cut pacing to the narration's energy (see FORMATS cut rhythm).
3. Don't move to audio until the loops are airtight.

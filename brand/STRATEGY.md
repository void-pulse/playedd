# Playedd — Growth Strategy (early phase)

How we grow the channel right now. Operational companion to CHANNEL.md (what the channel is) and FORMATS.md (how episodes are built). Priority: get monetized (subs, views, retention) without dropping the quality bar.

## Positioning (recap)
The hidden why behind everyday things, reveal-flavored wonder, NOT cynicism. Lane skews human: the mind, perception, memory, your own reality, "what is it actually like to be a person." Emotional register is **neutral + wonder** (leave them with "huh, that's amazing"), never poking self-esteem. See brand/VOICE.md.

## Flagship vein: "Why Are You...?"
Relatable everyday self-questions ("Why are you tired all the time?", "Why are you lazy?", "Why can't you remember being a baby?"). Huge search volume, endlessly bingeable, peer-level not guru. **REGISTER RULE:** reframe any "flaw" as a fascinating mechanism ("you're not lazy, your brain saves energy"), never shaming. Validated: Zenn's "Why You Can't Remember Being a Baby" did 1.7M in this exact shape.

## Growth rules (the actual plan)
1. **The dead zone is normal.** New channels get near-zero views for weeks. Growth is non-linear; you win on ONE breakout, not steady progress. Don't read into early numbers. Judge the strategy around video 15-20, not video 6 (the Zenn pattern: 15 videos, 1-2 carry the channel).
2. **Relatability beats authority early, and we already win here.** Our videos sell curiosity, not credentials, so we sidestep the "low-views-next-to-an-expert" social-proof trap. Stay relatable; tone = curious peer, not professor.
3. **Shorts are the MAIN early growth engine,** not a side dish. The shorts feed is hook-driven, not subscriber/authority-gated, so it's how a no-name channel gets discovered and banks subs fast. **Every long-form spawns 2-4 teaser shorts**, each a different angle (hook / head-fake / one surprising beat / payoff), released on a fixed cadence: the FIRST short drops launch-day, ~2 hours after the main video (ride the launch); the rest trickle every 2 days after. Teasers WITHHOLD the payoff and drive to the full video (see ep5's short).
4. **Double down on winners.** The moment a video breaks out, make 3 more exactly like it, same vein and format. Do not wander off a winner.
5. **Cadence / release schedule (locked 2026-06-09):** **2 long-form per week, Tuesday + Saturday, ~2pm ET**, scheduled in advance from the buffer (same days + time every week so the audience and algorithm learn it). Plus **3-5 shorts/week** sprinkled between, especially on non-upload days, each teasing a full video. Publish a few hours before US evening prime so early engagement lands at peak. Go to a 3rd weekly long-form ONLY once the pipeline runs with no babysitting and quality holds. Re-check the exact time against YouTube Analytics ("when your viewers are on YouTube") after ~2-4 weeks of data. Never delete/re-upload a published video (resets the algorithm).
6. **Packaging carries it:** title = plain question (≤6 words), thumbnail = one bold caps question + one doodle (the title/thumbnail one-two punch). **Always produce 3 DISTINCT thumbnail options per video for A/B testing** (different hook/visual/caps, not minor variants). The packaging must beat the low view count on curiosity alone.

## What does NOT apply
Personal-brand tactics (document my journey, show my face/insecurities, "my first 30 days"). We're faceless and topic-driven. Skip them.

## Build reminders (for the worker)
- ~8 min long-form (Zenn's sweet spot; too short and too long both underperform). Hook lands in the first 2 seconds.
- Neutral-wonder register; for any "flaw"/health topic, reveal the mechanism, no advice/shaming, no clinical mental-health.
- Frame-exact image timing (pin input fps to output fps; no cumulative drift).
- End cards: MAIN video = silent like/subscribe outro, lower-left only, CENTER + TOP-RIGHT kept clear for Logan's YouTube end-screen elements. SHORT = Playedd logo + up-arrow + "FOR FULL BREAKDOWN."

## Animation (selective — capability proven 2026-06-09)
Stan can now be ANIMATED (motion via Kling image-to-video on fal: doodle stills → motion, stitched with Jack VO). Proven with a "day in the life of Stan" test (test/stan_demo/). Use it SELECTIVELY, not everywhere:
- **Best ROI:** animate SHORTS (the shorts feed rewards motion, and shorts are our discovery engine) and the COLD-OPEN HOOK of long-form (the first 2 seconds decide the video; animated Stan acting out the scenario beats a static frame), plus 2-3 key Stan "acting" beats per episode.
- **Keep static:** explanatory / metaphor / diagram frames (concepts read clearer still) and most mid-episode beats. Do NOT animate a whole 8-min episode.
- **Cost** scales with SECONDS animated, not much with tier: standard ~$0.05/s, Pro ~$0.07/s, 4K ~2-3x. 4K is wasted on flat doodles, use standard or Pro at 1080p. Selective use = a few dollars per episode.
- **Why it matters:** Zenn and ThatGuy are mostly static; an animated recurring Stan makes us stand out and leverages our character. No lip-sync needed (Stan's mouth is a line; gesture + VO sells the "talking").

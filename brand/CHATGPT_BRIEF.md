## The channel
- **Playedd** — a "hidden why" curiosity channel: we take something ordinary and reveal the strange, true reason behind it. Niche = psychology, science, history, human behavior, the hidden design of everyday life.
- Tagline: **"Daily wonders · Weekly deep dives."** Subscribers are called **"the Stans"** (CTA = "Become a Stan"); mascot is **Stan**, a plain crude stick figure (round white head, two dot eyes, simple line mouth, thin limbs, no hair).
- Small but growing channel. Shorts get hundreds-to-thousands of views; long-form is the weak spot we're fixing.

## Two formats
**1) Shorts (daily, the top-of-funnel).** 15–30s vertical 9:16.
**2) Long-form episodes (weekly deep dives).** 6–9 min, 16:9.
Operating rule: **shorts are a cheap test bed. A topic only earns a long-form episode after a short on it overperforms.** Analytics pick the winners; the winners get the expensive deep-dive treatment.

## SHORTS — style & formula (analytics-proven)
- **Winning topic molds:** (a) relatable **"Why does [familiar everyday thing in YOUR life] actually [surprising truth]?"** (popcorn pricing, casino time, milk-at-back-of-store — these hit 700–1,400 views); (b) **myth-busters** "you think it's X… it's actually Y" (fossil fuels = plankton not dinosaurs); (c) occasional **skits** (a little comedic scene + a wonder payoff). Leaning more into **psychology-of-people** ("why you remember insults longer than compliments," the spotlight effect) because self-relevant = most "reel-in."
- **What flops:** abstract trivia with no personal hook.
- **Hook:** scroll-stopping in the first 1–2 seconds, NEVER "did you know." Then escalate with a second jaw-dropper, land a repeatable kicker, **end on the kicker** (end on a strong concrete word, never a weak word like "it/can/them" or the AI voice sounds unfinished).
- **Visual style:** bright, flat, hand-drawn doodle (thick black outlines, flat colors). Each scene is ONE bold idea. Art is **text-free** — burned-in **CapCut-style captions** (ONE word at a time, big, white with thick black outline) carry every word. Phone-safe (nothing important in the top ~15% or bottom ~12%). 4–8 scenes; picture-rich (6–8) is preferred.
- **End card (last ~2.5s):** the Playedd stamp logo, a red "DAILY WONDER #N" streak badge, "BECOME A STAN," and a hand-drawn red circle around where the @name/subscribe sits.
- **Pinned comment formula:** a bonus jaw-drop tied to the video → "Become a Stan, one every day" → a comment trigger ("drop a 'wait, that's true?' fact, I might make it a short" / "what's the worst X you've done?"). Comments are a ranking signal.
- **Publishing:** post natively, one a day, spaced; don't dump several at once.

## LONG-FORM — style & process (modeled on the channel "Zenn")
- **PACKAGING FIRST (the #1 lever):** design the **title + thumbnail before the script.** Titles are curiosity bombs (e.g. "Your Brain Deleted Your First Years," not "Why Can't You Remember Being a Baby"). Thumbnails are **cinematic, high-contrast, one arresting image + 2–3 huge words.**
- **Visual style:** **MOODY CINEMATIC** doodles — dark near-black backgrounds, one dramatic spotlight, high contrast (a deliberate step up from the bright shorts look). Text-free art. A visual punch every ~15–20s; several are "thumbnail moments."
- **Structure (Zenn formula):** impossible question (0–30s) → unsettling fact → the science → a paradox → philosophical implications → identity crisis → eerie landing. **Re-hook before every slow turn.**
- **Writing:** SHORT sentences (people watch, don't read). **Storytelling anchors** — open on a real person/scene (a specific anecdote is the stickiest beat), one concrete sensory image per section. Mystery → reveal → twist. Aim "fast fascinating-essay + vivid examples," not slow narrative.
- **Voice/pacing:** measured, slightly slower delivery; dramatic pauses before reveals.
- **Subtitles** (CC track) + **end screen** (subscribe + "best for viewer" video) on every episode.

## The strategy / why long-form was failing
- Shorts and long-form are **separate audiences and algorithms** — shorts success doesn't transfer. Our long-form got near-zero views because (1) packaging (title/thumbnail) was generic and neglected, (2) episodes were too short/shallow (4–6 min skims vs 8–13 min deep dives), (3) retention was low. The niche is proven (Zenn does millions on nearly our exact topics); we just hadn't built a real long-form channel yet. The fix = packaging-first + deeper + retention-engineered + the moody style.

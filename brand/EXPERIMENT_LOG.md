# Playedd — Experiment Log (the evidence machine)

NOT a strategy doc. This is the measurement tool the strategy freeze requires. Strategy is frozen at v1.0 (`IDENTITY.md`) for ~10 uploads — this log is how we let reality attack it. Next teacher is YouTube, not us.

## How to use
Every upload is an experiment. Two jobs:
1. **Before publish** — score the 4 emotions (0–10 each, gut call) and note the pillar + the identity-shift button line. This is the *hypothesis*.
2. **After ~7 days live** — fill the 5 metrics from YouTube Studio. This is the *result*.

Over time we're not asking "did this video work?" — we're asking **"which emotion works?"** The point is the pattern across rows, not any single row.

**The most important column is "Again?"** — a binary Yes/No: *would I make this kind of video again?* Judged on **identity, not views.** Wombat cubes at 1,200 views = No (not who we are). Time-speeds-up at 400 = Yes (exactly who we are). This is the guard against drifting toward whatever random fact temporarily wins. Playedd should be a channel people *subscribe to*, not one they *accidentally encounter*.

## The 5 metrics (ignore everything else, especially raw views)
| Metric | Goal | Where |
|---|---|---|
| **Subscribers gained / video** ⭐ | identity Shorts > discovery Shorts | Studio → Audience (per video) |
| CTR | >5% | Studio → Reach |
| 30-sec retention | >70% | Studio → Engagement (audience retention) |
| Avg view duration (%) | >50% | Studio → Engagement |
| Shares | rising vs last | Studio → Engagement |
| Comments | self-identification ("I do this") | read the comments |

⭐ **Subs-per-video is the headline signal** — often overlooked by small channels. The real question isn't "does pain win?" but **"does pain create SUBSCRIBERS?"** A short can pull 2,500 views + 3 subs (discovery) while another pulls 1,100 views + 21 subs (identity). The second one wins. Views are reach; subs are affinity.

WATCH (don't formalize yet — just observe whether the data splits this way): two emerging short types — **Discovery Shorts** (sharks, mirror flip, never-touched) get *shared* by strangers; **Identity Shorts** (one insult, awkward silence, 2AM cringe) get *subscribed* to. If subs/video confirms it, that's a real distinction.

Caveat: below a few hundred impressions these are statistically noisy. Don't overreact to one video. Look for patterns once ~10 rows exist.

## Tone guardrail (don't overcorrect into doom)
Pain = **friction, not sadness.** ✅ "why one insult ruins your day / why awkward silences hurt / why waiting for a text feels awful." ❌ "why everyone is lonely / why life is meaningless." Keep it curious, not heavy. Target emotional mix ≈ **60% recognition · 25% wonder · 10% discomfort · 5% existential.** All-existential-dread narrows the audience hard.

## The identity-shift bar (every video)
Final sentence must induce "I'll never see that the same way." If a topic can't support that ending, it isn't a Playedd video. (e.g. childhood amnesia → "the first chapter of your life belongs to someone you'll never meet.")

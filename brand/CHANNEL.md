# Channel Bible — Playedd

> The source of truth for what this channel is. If a video doesn't fit this, don't make it.

## Name
**Playedd** (stylized with the double D, like "Zenn" doubles its N). Pronounced "played."
Tagline: **"you're being played. here's how."**

## The one-sentence pitch
Short, true, weirdly satisfying breakdowns of how the world is quietly getting you: the scams, the hidden business models, the pricing and psychology tricks, and who's actually profiting.

## Why this niche
It's the rare overlap of *broadly appealing* and *well-paying*. Everyone has money and everyone hates being fooled, so the audience is universal (a 19-year-old and a retiree both click "why popcorn costs $9"). But unlike pure psychology or trivia channels, this lane is finance-adjacent, so advertisers actually bid for it (~$8-15 RPM vs $3-7 for psychology/history). Maximum reach and good pay in the same theme.

The channel stands entirely on the quality of the videos. No creator-background angle, no gimmick. Just genuinely fascinating "I never knew that" content people screenshot and send to a friend.

## Target viewer
Curious adults, broad age range, who like Half as Interesting / MagnatesMedia / ColdFusion energy. People who fall down Wikipedia holes and love feeling slightly smarter than the system.

## The visual hook
Crude, intentionally bad MS-Paint doodles (stick figures, wobbly lines, red stamps). Cheap, fast, instantly recognizable, and charming. The doodles carry the brand; the substance carries the channel.

## Non-negotiables (keep the channel alive)
- Every video discloses synthetic/AI content (YouTube Studio checkbox + a line in the description).
- Every video cites real sources in the description. The research is the moat; show it.
- Never publish two consecutive videos with the same title structure, thumbnail layout, or cold open. Rotate pillars (see FORMATS.md).
- Add a genuine take or a piece of context the Wikipedia summary wouldn't give. This is the line between "value YouTube can identify" and the AI slop that gets terminated.
- Accuracy over speed. One viral-for-being-wrong video is a reputation tax you don't want.

## Monetization path
- Phase 0 (now to 1,000 subs / 4,000 watch hours): ship consistently, find the breakout pillar, sharpen.
- Phase 1 (YPP approved): AdSense on. This lane's RPM ($8-15) means real money per view, unlike the psychology basement.
- Phase 2: affiliate (finance/consumer products fit this audience perfectly), plus a newsletter you own. The email list is the durable asset; AdSense is the floor, not the ceiling.

## Production model & cadence
Low-volume, high-production, longer-form. That is the proven model in this niche, and it's what the reference channel does. Depth beats frequency: one strong, deep episode does more than three thin ones.
- Aim for **~1 strong episode per week**, not 3 forgettable ones.
- Longer-form (see brand/FORMATS.md: 6-9 min and trending up) is where this lane performs and pays.
- Daily identical uploads is the exact fingerprint that got AI channels mass-terminated in early 2026. Variety, depth, and substance first; volume never at the cost of the human layer or the humor.

## Category
**Education** (confirmed; matches the reference channel). Set it on every upload. Leave the Education academic sub-fields (Type, Problems, Academic system, Level, Exam/course/standard) blank, they're for formal coursework, not explainers.

## Reference channel
**Zenn** (@zenn0009) — ~14 videos, ~136K subscribers, ~1M average views, ~13-minute doodle explainers, Education category. Proof that low-volume, high-production, longer-form doodle explainers work in this exact lane. Study how they do it; don't copy their topics.

## Localization
- YouTube **auto-dubbing ON**, with review-before-publish. Original spoken language is **English** on every upload.
- Do **not** hand-translate titles, descriptions, or tags yet. Revisit localized metadata only when analytics show real dubbed-language viewership.

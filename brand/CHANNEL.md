# Channel Bible — Playedd

> The source of truth for what this channel is. If a video doesn't fit this, don't make it.

## Name
**Playedd** (stylized with the double D, like "Zenn" doubles its N). Pronounced "played."
Tagline: **"the hidden why."** The name stays; the old "you're being played" box is gone. It was too cynical and too narrow for where this is going.
End stamp: the **Playedd logo, silent.** "YOU'RE BEING PLAYED" is retired as both tagline and on-screen stamp.

## The one-sentence pitch
Short, true, weirdly satisfying answers to questions you never thought to ask about everyday things: what color the sky was before anyone had a word for blue, why you can't remember being a baby, why everything ends in .99. The hidden why behind the ordinary. Reveal-flavored wonder, not cynicism.

Manipulation (scams, hidden business models, pricing and psychology tricks, who profits) is now ONE vein inside this, not the whole identity. The "money vein" keeps earning its place; it just shares the channel with deep time, scale, perception, and language.

## Why this niche
Universal curiosity is the largest audience on YouTube. Everyone has looked up at the sky, lost their own earliest memories, felt time speed up as they age. A 19-year-old and a retiree both click "what color was the sky before we had a word for blue." That reach is what the wonder lane buys, and it's why the model channel averages ~1M views on ~14 uploads. The game here is the breakout: this lane is built on one or two videos that go huge, not on uniform hits.

The honest tradeoff: pure wonder, science, and history pay a lower RPM (~$3-7) than the old finance-adjacent angle (~$8-15). We take that trade, because reach and shareability in this lane dwarf the RPM gap, and because the **money vein** (pricing, hidden business models, who profits) stays in rotation as the higher-RPM sub-lane. Big reach from wonder, better pay from the money vein, same channel.

The channel stands entirely on the quality of the videos. No creator-background angle, no gimmick. Just genuinely fascinating "I never knew that" content people screenshot and send to a friend.

## Target viewer
Curious adults, broad age range, who like Half as Interesting / MagnatesMedia / ColdFusion energy. People who fall down Wikipedia holes and love feeling slightly smarter than the system.

## The visual hook
Crude, intentionally bad MS-Paint doodles (stick figures, wobbly lines, red marks). Cheap, fast, instantly recognizable, and charming. The doodles carry the brand; the substance carries the channel.

The bar just went up, and this is the single biggest production upgrade: **one bold visual metaphor per frame.** Iconic, high-contrast, meaningful. NOT literal scene-clutter. One idea drawn big and clear beats a busy doodle every time. See brand/IMAGE_STYLE.md.

## Non-negotiables (keep the channel alive)
- Every video discloses synthetic/AI content (YouTube Studio checkbox + a line in the description).
- Every video cites real sources in the description. The research is the moat; show it.
- Never publish two consecutive videos with the same title structure, thumbnail layout, or cold open. Rotate veins (see FORMATS.md).
- Add a genuine take or a piece of context the Wikipedia summary wouldn't give. This is the line between "value YouTube can identify" and the AI slop that gets terminated.
- Accuracy over speed. One viral-for-being-wrong video is a reputation tax you don't want.

## Monetization path
- Phase 0 (now to 1,000 subs / 4,000 watch hours): ship consistently, find the breakout vein, sharpen.
- Phase 1 (YPP approved): AdSense on. This lane's RPM ($8-15) means real money per view, unlike the psychology basement.
- Phase 2: affiliate (finance/consumer products fit this audience perfectly), plus a newsletter you own. The email list is the durable asset; AdSense is the floor, not the ceiling.

## Production model & cadence
Low-volume, high-production, longer-form. That is the proven model in this niche, and it's what the reference channel does. Depth beats frequency: one strong, deep episode does more than three thin ones.
- Aim for **~1 strong episode per week**, not 3 forgettable ones.
- Longer-form (see brand/FORMATS.md: 6-9 min and trending up) is where this lane performs and pays.
- Daily identical uploads is the exact fingerprint that got AI channels mass-terminated in early 2026. Variety, depth, and substance first; volume never at the cost of the human layer or the humor.

## Reference channel (MECHANICS ONLY, do not copy)
**Zenn** (@zenn0009) — ~14 videos, ~136K subscribers, ~1M average views, ~13-minute explainers, Education category. Proof that low-volume, high-production, longer-form explainers built on one or two breakouts work in this exact lane.

Steal the **formula**, never the topics (his are now saturated): anchor on a universal everyday action, zoom out to deep time or huge scale, reveal that "normal" is recent, engineered, or stranger than you assumed, and wrap it in a bold one-idea thumbnail with a short punchy question.

## Localization
- YouTube **auto-dubbing ON**, with review-before-publish. Original spoken language is **English** on every upload.
- Do **not** hand-translate titles, descriptions, or tags yet. Revisit localized metadata only when analytics show real dubbed-language viewership.

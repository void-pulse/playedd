# Prompt 01 — Idea Generation

Use this in Claude Code (or paste to Claude) when you want video ideas. Output goes in an episode's `00_idea.md`.

---

You are the head of research for **Playedd**, a YouTube channel doing crude hand-drawn explainers about how the world is quietly getting you: scams, hidden business models, pricing and psychology tricks, and who really profits. Read `brand/CHANNEL.md` and `brand/FORMATS.md` first. Check `content/IDEAS.md` so you don't duplicate the backlog.

Generate **10 video ideas**. For each idea give:
1. **Pillar** (one of the five from FORMATS.md: Follow The Money / The Psychology Trick / The Scam Explained / Who Owns This / Hidden Cost)
2. **Working title** (use a title structure that fits the pillar; vary structures across the 10)
3. **The hook** (the one-sentence cold open that makes someone stop scrolling)
4. **Why it's true and sourceable** (name the kind of primary source: company filings, court records, FTC actions, academic studies, industry reporting, etc.)
5. **The human-layer beat** (the one non-obvious take, connection, or myth-correction that separates this from a Wikipedia summary)
6. **Estimated runtime** (vary: some 6 min, some 10-12)

Hard requirements:
- Real, verifiable topics only. No invented "facts." If you're not confident it's real, flag it.
- Spread across at least 3 different pillars.
- Favor topics with broad appeal (money, everyday products, common experiences) and strong visual-gag potential, the doodle style shines when there's something absurd to draw.
- Avoid the most over-covered takes unless you have a genuinely fresh angle.

End with your single strongest pick and a one-line reason.

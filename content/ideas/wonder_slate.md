## Per-episode notes

### ep5 ★ Why Do You Hate Photos of Yourself? [YOM] — VERIFIED
- **Question:** You look great in the mirror, then awful in the photo. Why?
- **Payoff (escalating):** 1) Mere-exposure: you've seen your *reversed* mirror face your whole life, so the true photo looks subtly wrong (Mita 1977: you prefer your mirror image, friends prefer your photo). 2) Lens distortion: a 12-inch selfie makes your nose look ~6% longer, nose-to-chin ratio ~17% bigger (UT Southwestern / JAMA Facial Plastic Surgery 2018). 3) A mirror is live/moving; a photo freezes one frame.
- **MYTH-RISK:** do NOT call the mirror "the real you." It's reversed and the version you're biased toward. A well-shot photo at distance is closer to how others see you. Frame comfort as *familiarity*, not accuracy.

### ep6 Why Is Every World Map Wrong? [ED]
- **Question:** Why is Greenland drawn as big as Africa when it's 14x smaller?
- **Payoff:** Mercator (1569) distorts area to keep angles true for navigation; the closer to the poles, the more it inflates. A 450-year-old design choice, not a mistake. Show the true-size morph.
- **Verify at script:** area ratios, alternative projections.

### ep7 Why Don't Clouds Just Fall? [HS]
- **Question:** A cloud weighs as much as ~100 elephants. Why is it floating?
- **Payoff:** the water is spread as tiny droplets across a huge volume; they fall so slowly (and updrafts hold them) that they effectively float. Weight is distributed, not a block.
- **Verify at script:** the ~1.1M lb average cumulus figure + droplet terminal velocity.

### ep8 ★ Why Does Your Voice Sound Weird? [YOM] — VERIFIED
- **Question:** Why does your recorded voice sound like a stranger?
- **Payoff:** you hear yourself via air + bone conduction; bone conduction boosts the lows, so your live voice sounds deeper/fuller than it is. A recording is air-only, so it sounds higher/thinner, and that's what everyone else hears. Bonus: "voice confrontation" (Holzman & Rousey 1966).
- **MYTH-RISK:** don't say the recording is the "true" voice and the perceived one is "fake." Two filters. Say bone conduction "emphasizes" lows, not "adds bass." Safest topic on the list.

### ep9 Is Water Older Than the Sun? [DT]
- **Question:** How old is the water you just drank?
- **Payoff:** much of Earth's water predates the Sun, formed in interstellar clouds; deuterium ratios point to a pre-solar origin. You're drinking 4.6B+ year old material on a loop.
- **Risk: MED.** Real but nuanced ("a significant fraction," not "all"). State precisely, like the blue ep. Verify Cleeves et al. 2014.

### ep10 Why Does the Moon Look So Big? [HS]
- **Question:** Why is the moon giant on the horizon and tiny overhead, at the same size?
- **Payoff:** the Moon illusion. Not magnification (photos prove identical size); it's your brain. Leading theories still debated = honesty beat. Try the upside-down-look trick on camera.
- **Verify at script:** present competing theories fairly.

### ep11 Why Do You Forget Why You Walked In? [YOM]
- **Question:** You walked in with a purpose and it's gone. Why?
- **Payoff:** the doorway effect / event boundaries. Crossing a doorway makes your brain file the old context and start a new "event," dumping the goal (Radvansky). Huge comment bait.
- **Verify at script:** Radvansky studies; note replication nuance.

### ep12 Were High Heels Made for Men? [DT]
- **Question:** Who were heels actually invented for?
- **Payoff:** Persian cavalry men (heels hooked into stirrups for archery); European aristocratic men adopted them as status; women's fashion came later, men dropped them. A clean "it used to be the opposite" flip.
- **Verify at script:** Bata Shoe Museum / Elizabeth Semmelhack.

### ep13 Why Do Humans Have White Eyes? [YOM]
- **Question:** Why do we show the whites of our eyes when almost no other animal does?
- **Payoff:** the cooperative eye hypothesis. Visible sclera lets others track your gaze, which only pays off in a species that cooperates and reads intentions.
- **Verify at script:** cooperative eye hypothesis (Tomasello); note it's a leading hypothesis.

### ep14 Where's the Loneliest Place on Earth? [HS]
- **Question:** What's the farthest you can get from any other human?
- **Payoff:** Point Nemo, the oceanic pole of inaccessibility; nearest land ~1,670 mi, so the nearest humans are often astronauts overhead on the ISS. Reveal: it's the "spacecraft cemetery" where agencies crash dead satellites. Two payoffs.
- **Verify at script:** ISS-overhead framing; spacecraft cemetery.

# 🧠 BRAINSTORM v2 — deep-think expansion (don't limit ourselves)
Fresh topics beyond the Inspiration dump. Short titles, the gap, and the one-line payoff. ★ = strong promotion candidate (high ceiling, simple, safe, NOT Zenn's). Verify all at script time.

### Your Own Mind / Body [YOM] — our CTR engine
- ★ **Why Do You Go Blind and Not Notice?** — saccadic masking: your brain deletes the blur every time your eyes dart, and fakes the gap. You're "blind" minutes a day.
- ★ **Why Can't You Smell Your Own House?** — sensory adaptation; your nose mutes constant smells (and what strangers smell).
- ★ **Why Can You Smell Rain Coming?** — geosmin; humans detect it better than sharks smell blood.
- ★ **Why Do You Forget Your Dreams?** — you lose ~95% within minutes; the brain suppresses dream memory.
- **Why Can't You Read in a Dream?** — text and clocks won't hold still (the reality-check trick).
- **Why Do Songs Get Stuck in Your Head?** — earworms; the brain loops unfinished patterns.
- ★ **Why Do You Cringe at Old Memories?** — random intrusive memory; the brain's error-replay.
- **Why Do You Get Déjà Vu?** — a memory misfire that tags "now" as "before."
- **Don't You Notice Your Own Nose?** — it's always in view; your brain edits it out.
- ★ **Can Some People Not Picture Anything?** — aphantasia; the blank mind's eye (and no inner voice).
- **Why Do Onions Make You Cry?** — chemical self-defense; the plant fights back.
- **Why Do You Get Brain Freeze?** — referred pain; your brain panicking about cold.
- **Why Do You Hiccup?** — a leftover reflex from our amphibian past.
- **Why Do Goosebumps Happen to Music?** — frisson; a vestigial fur reflex.
- **Why Does Your Stomach Drop on a Drop?** — free-fall scrambles your gut sensors.

### Deep Time / History [DT] — "how recent / how old"
- ★ **Is a Day Really 24 Hours?** — Earth's spin is slowing; dinosaurs had ~23-hour days; days are still getting longer.
- ★ **Why Is an Hour 60 Minutes?** — we still run on 4,000-year-old Babylonian base-60.
- ★ **Why Is a Week 7 Days?** — the only time unit with no astronomical reason.
- **Why Do Clocks Go Clockwise?** — a frozen sundial shadow from the northern hemisphere.
- ★ **Why Is North "Up" on Maps?** — totally arbitrary; old maps pointed east.
- ★ **Was Orange Named After the Fruit?** — yes; English had no word for the color until the fruit arrived (ties to the parked blue idea, done simply).
- ★ **Did T-Rex or Stegosaurus Live Closer to Us?** — us, by 20M+ years. Deep time is bigger than you think.
- ★ **Are Sharks Older Than Trees?** — yes. Older than Saturn's rings, too.
- ★ **Were the Pyramids Ancient to Cleopatra?** — she lived closer to us than to the people who built them.
- **Why Do We Say "Hello"?** — the telephone invented the greeting.

### Hidden Scale / Physics / Cosmos [HS]
- ★ **Why Don't You Feel the Earth Spinning?** — you're moving ~1,000 mph right now and feel nothing.
- ★ **How Much Does the Air Weigh on You?** — about a ton, pressing on you this second.
- ★ **Stars or Grains of Sand: Which Wins?** — stars, by a landslide.
- ★ **Are There More Trees or Stars?** — more trees on Earth than stars in the Milky Way.
- ★ **Are You Made of Dead Stars?** — literally; your atoms were forged in stars.
- ★ **Did You Breathe Caesar's Last Breath?** — almost certainly; the math of one breath.
- ★ **How Much Does the Internet Weigh?** — about a strawberry, in electrons.
- ★ **What Color Is the Universe?** — beige. "Cosmic latte."
- ★ **Why Is the Night Sky Dark?** — Olbers' paradox; infinite stars should make it blinding.
- **What Color Is a Mirror?** — not silver; faintly green.
- **Why Can't You See Most Colors?** — visible light is a sliver of what's out there.
- **Why Is the Ocean Blue if Water Is Clear?** — and it's not just the sky's reflection.

### Engineered Defaults / design + money [ED] — ~1 in 5
- ★ **Do "Close Door" Buttons Work?** — placebo buttons; most do nothing. (Crosswalk buttons too.)
- ★ **Why Does Everything End in .99?** — the penny that rewires your brain (money vein).
- **Why Is Ketchup So Hard to Pour?** — it's a solid until you hit it right (0.045 mph).
- **Why Are Golf Balls Dimpled?** — a smooth ball flies half as far.
- **Why Are Tires Black?** — rubber is naturally white.
- **Why Are Barns Red?** — cheap rust, and dying stars made the iron.
- ★ **Why Do We Still Make Pennies?** — each one costs more than it's worth (money vein).
- ★ **Where Did Car Colors Go?** — cars used to be every color; now ~80% are grayscale.
- **Why Are School Buses Yellow?** — engineered for your peripheral vision.

## Added 2026-06-09
- ★ **How Can You Understand Something but Not Explain It?** [Your Own Mind] — Logan idea. Tacit knowledge: you can ride a bike, recognize a face, sense a vibe, and still not put it into words. The brain knows more than it can say.
- ★ **How Are You So Good at Recognizing Faces?** [Your Own Mind] — Logan idea. You can ID thousands of faces in an instant (but blank on their names). The brain has dedicated face hardware (fusiform face area); ties to pareidolia.
- ★ **Are We Living in a Simulation?** (alt: "What If None of This Is Real?") [Hidden Scale / big existential] — Logan idea, the "are we in the Matrix" question, done concrete: the real argument (Bostrom's simulation trilemma, statistically you're more likely a simulated mind than a real one), the "evidence" people cite (the universe has a speed limit + a smallest possible size, like pixels; quantum weirdness only resolving when observed), and the honest catch (basically unfalsifiable). Neutral mind-bender; pairs with the aliens ep in the cosmic/existential vein. MYTH-RISK: present as a fascinating argument, NOT proven; be upfront it's untestable.
- ⚠️→★ **Will a Robot Take Your Job?** (alt: "Will Anyone Have a Job in the Future?") [future / history] — Logan idea. STRONG if framed as HISTORY + reassurance, weak/risky if framed as doom-prediction. Best version: the "machines will take all the jobs" panic is 200+ years old (Luddites smashing looms, then cars, calculators, computers); jobs transformed instead of vanishing (most jobs today didn't exist a century ago; the "lump of labor fallacy"); then the honest, calm "but is AI the next loom, or actually different this time?" Neutral, evergreen, less anxiety.
  - CUT the "how to get rich / how to make money" angle — that's the grift/finance-guru lane, off our neutral-wonder brand, edges into financial advice, and dates fast. Don't.
  - RISK: pure prediction dates fast (we want evergreen); job-loss fear cuts against don't-upset-people. Keep it calm + historical, not doom.
  - Evergreen cousins already in slate: "Why Is the 40-Hour Work Week...", "What Did People Do Before Jobs?"
- ⚠️ **Is Elon Musk a Genius?** [figure-as-anchor] — RISKY as a straight verdict on a living, polarizing figure: it's political, it ages fast, and it invites flame-war comments, all off our neutral-wonder lane. SAFER reframe that keeps the juice: **"Why Do We Call People Geniuses?"** / "What Actually Makes a Genius?" using Elon, Jobs, etc. as EXAMPLES, not a verdict, the halo effect, why wealth/confidence reads as brilliance, the different kinds of "smart" (inventor vs visionary vs marketer). Pairs with the Einstein episode.
- ★ **Could Aliens Actually Reach Us?** [Hidden Scale / cosmic] — the distance + lightspeed + tech barrier. Covers: how far habitable worlds likely are; the brutal scale (nearest star ~4.2 light-years; even AT light speed = years; our fastest craft = tens of thousands of years); the tech level an alien civilization would need; the Fermi paradox + Drake-equation odds; and how laughably far WE are from leaving (Voyager after ~50 years isn't even a light-day out), plus when we might. Spin-off episode: "What Would Aliens Actually Look Like?" (more speculative).

## 🔬 Researched — ready to script

### 1. Why Everything You Own Is Designed to Die  → in production as ep4
- The play: planned obsolescence, a century deep. The 1924 Phoebus cartel is the ORIGIN reveal; the payoff is how the same idea runs your life today — phone throttling, printer-ink lockouts, car-feature subscriptions, and repair lockouts. The trick stopped breaking your things and started keeping them from you.
- Killer fact: Dec 23 1924, Geneva — Osram, Philips, GE and others secretly agreed to cut bulb life from 2,500h to 1,000h and fined any member whose bulbs lasted longer. The Livermore CA fire-station bulb has burned since 1901. Modern echoes: Apple "batterygate," HP "Dynamic Security" ink blocks, Mercedes ~$1,200/yr horsepower, John Deere repair lockouts.
- Sources: IEEE Spectrum "The Great Lightbulb Conspiracy"; Wikipedia "Phoebus cartel"; US v. GE (1949); Bernard London (1932); Arizona AG (Apple); HP CEO Enrique Lores (Davos 2024); right-to-repair coverage.

### 2. The Man Who Invented Manipulation (Edward Bernays)
- The play: the origin of modern advertising/PR — manufacturing desire around your reason.
- Killer fact: 1929 "Torches of Freedom" — Bernays staged women lighting Lucky Strikes at the NYC Easter parade as fake feminist protest; women's cigarette share went 5%→12% (1923–29). Same man invented the American bacon-and-eggs breakfast for a pork client.
- Sources: History Today "The Original Influencer"; Wikipedia "PR campaigns of Edward Bernays."

### 3. The Sugar Cover-Up
- The play: an industry bought the science to dodge the blame for heart disease.
- Killer fact: the Sugar Research Foundation paid 3 Harvard scientists ~$50k (today's money) for a 1967 review steering blame to fat, not sugar; one author, Mark Hegsted, later helped write the federal dietary guidelines.
- Sources: JAMA Internal Medicine (Kearns et al. 2016); NYT; NPR.

### 4. The Holiday That Destroyed Its Own Creator (Mother's Day)
- The play: a pure, personal day hijacked into a multibillion-dollar retail machine.
- Killer fact: Anna Jarvis invented Mother's Day, then spent her fortune trying to get it rescinded and died penniless in a sanitarium (1948); she once dumped a "Mother's Day Salad" on a restaurant floor; a florist may have paid her medical bills.
- Sources: Washington Post; Britannica "Anna Jarvis"; National Geographic.

### 5. From Trash to Treasure (Lobster)
- The play: "luxury" is just a story someone sold you — the product never changed.
- Killer fact: colonists called lobster "the cockroach of the sea," fed it to prisoners/servants, used it as fertilizer; shells in the yard signaled poverty; ~11¢/lb lobster vs 53¢/lb baked beans in early-1800s Boston. (Skip the "servants revolted" contract myth.)
- Sources: Chowhound; LobsterAnywhere; History Facts.

### 6. The Toy That Tricked America (Beanie Babies)
- The play: manufactured scarcity — fake shortage on a mass-produced item.
- Killer fact: Ty Warner "retired" toys while pumping out millions; eBay moved $500M of Beanie Babies in ONE month in 1997; prices later fell 98%. Same playbook as today's sneakers/Labubu/NFTs.
- Sources: The Hustle; Mental Floss; Braithwaite.

### 7. You've Probably Never Tasted Real Olive Oil (Agromafia)
- The play: organized crime cuts and mislabels cheap oil as premium "extra virgin."
- Killer fact: a 2010 UC Davis study found 69% of imported samples failed extra-virgin standards; 60 Minutes pegged Italy's "agromafia" food fraud at ~$16B/yr. NOTE: the exact "% fake" figure is contested — flag it openly (on-brand for our sourcing).
- Sources: 60 Minutes (2016); UC Davis Olive Center (2010); Tom Mueller "Extra Virginity."

### 8. The App That Profits When You Stay Single (Match Group)
- The play: the company that owns the dating apps makes money when you DON'T find love.
- Killer fact: Match Group owns Tinder/Hinge/OkCupid/etc.; a 2024 class action alleges the apps are gamified like gambling to keep users paying, despite Hinge's "designed to be deleted" slogan. (Frame as allegation — Match denies it.)
- Sources: CBS News; Jacobin; the Feb 2024 class action complaint.

### 9. The Disease Invented to Sell Cream (Cellulite)
- The play: a normal body texture pathologized into a "condition" to sell a cure.
- Killer fact: April 1968 Vogue was first English-language mag to print "cellulite," calling it the fat you couldn't lose before; a 1978 study called it an "invented disease"; the FTC has repeatedly sued cream-makers.
- Sources: Refinery29; Tyla; Wikipedia "Cellulite."

### 10. The Option You're Not Supposed to Pick (Decoy Effect)
- The play: a deliberately bad pricing option exists only to steer you to a pricier one.
- Killer fact: in Ariely's Economist test, a useless $125 print-only option pushed 84% to the expensive combo; removing it cut revenue from $11,444 to $8,012. Same trick as our popcorn-size video — natural callback.
- Sources: Ariely "Predictably Irrational"; The Economist "The importance of irrelevant alternatives."
